# Glossary — the alphabet soup, decoded

Billing departments speak in acronyms because it works: confused people pay.
When talking to the user, always expand these on first use.

## Codes on the bill

- **CPT code** (Current Procedural Terminology) — 5-digit code for every
  procedure/service (e.g., 99285 = highest-level ER visit). The unit of
  audit and price comparison.
- **HCPCS** — CMS's superset of CPT; Level II codes (letter + 4 digits)
  cover drugs, supplies, ambulance (e.g., J-codes for injectables).
- **ICD-10** — diagnosis codes (why you were treated). Mismatched ICD-10 ↔
  CPT pairs cause insurance denials.
- **DRG** (Diagnosis-Related Group) — bundled code for an entire inpatient
  stay; how Medicare and many insurers pay hospitals.
- **Revenue code** — 4-digit hospital department code (0450 = ER). On
  itemized hospital bills next to CPTs.
- **Modifier** — 2 characters appended to a CPT that change payment (e.g.,
  -25, -59). Modifier games are a common upcoding vector.

## Documents

- **Itemized bill / itemized statement** — every charge, line by line, with
  codes. What you must demand. A "summary bill" (one balance-due number) is
  not it.
- **EOB** (Explanation of Benefits) — the insurer's statement of what was
  billed, what they allowed, what they paid, and what you owe. **Not a
  bill.** Never pay a provider more than the EOB's "patient responsibility."
- **GFE** (Good Faith Estimate) — written cost estimate that self-pay/
  uninsured patients are entitled to before scheduled care (No Surprises
  Act). Bills more than $400 over the GFE are formally disputable.
- **Chargemaster / CDM** — the hospital's master list price file. Fictional
  retail prices; the anchor they hope you accept.
- **Standard charges file / MRF** (machine-readable file) — the price file
  hospitals must publish (Hospital Price Transparency Rule): gross charges,
  cash price, and payer-negotiated rates per code. Your best weapon.
- **UB-04 / CMS-1450** — the claim form hospitals send insurers. You can
  request it; it shows exactly what was claimed.
- **Superbill / CMS-1500** — the outpatient/professional equivalent.
- **FAP** (Financial Assistance Policy) — the charity-care policy every
  nonprofit hospital must maintain and publicize under IRS 501(r).

## Money words

- **Allowed amount** — what the insurer deems the service worth; the basis
  for your share. Billed charges above it get "adjusted" away in-network.
- **AGB** (Amounts Generally Billed) — 501(r) cap: FAP-eligible patients
  can't be charged more than what insurers generally pay. Cite it.
- **Deductible / copay / coinsurance / OOP max** — your cost-share stack:
  flat amounts (copay), the amount you pay before coverage kicks in
  (deductible), your percentage after that (coinsurance), and the annual
  ceiling on all of it (out-of-pocket maximum).
- **Balance billing** — provider bills you for the difference between their
  charge and the insurer's allowed amount. Illegal in-network; restricted
  out-of-network by the No Surprises Act in emergency/facility scenarios.
- **Facility fee** — separate hospital charge for using the building, often
  hundreds of dollars, increasingly attached to ordinary clinic visits
  because the clinic is hospital-owned. Frequently reducible or waivable on
  challenge, and several states restrict them.
- **Self-pay / cash price** — the price for paying without insurance.
  Sometimes lower than an insured patient's share. In the MRF.
- **Prompt-pay discount** — reduction for paying (a settled amount) quickly.
- **Financial hardship adjustment** — discretionary write-down short of full
  charity care. Ask for it by name.

## Billing failure modes

- **Upcoding** — billing a more severe (expensive) code than warranted.
- **Unbundling** — splitting one bundled procedure into separately billed
  parts.
- **Phantom billing** — charges for things that never happened.
- **Duplicate billing** — the same service billed twice.
- **Surprise bill** — out-of-network charge at an in-network facility or in
  an emergency; mostly banned by the No Surprises Act since 2022.

## Process words

- **PPDR** (Patient-Provider Dispute Resolution) — the federal process for
  disputing a bill that exceeds a GFE by $400+.
- **Prior authorization** — insurer pre-approval; missing it is a top denial
  reason, and often the provider's procedural fault, not yours.
- **ECA** (Extraordinary Collection Action) — lawsuits, liens, wage
  garnishment, credit reporting. Nonprofits must wait 120 days and check FAP
  eligibility before ECAs (501(r)).
- **Debt validation** — your FDCPA right to force a collector to prove the
  debt within 30 days of first contact.
- **Statute of limitations** — state-law deadline for suing over a debt
  (often 3–6 years). Payments or written acknowledgment can restart it.
- **Charge-off / re-aging** — accounting and credit-report timing terms that
  collectors abuse; re-aging a debt's delinquency date is illegal.
